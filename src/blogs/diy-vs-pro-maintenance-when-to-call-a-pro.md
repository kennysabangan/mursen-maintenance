# DIY vs Professional Maintenance: When to Call a Pro

Maintaining a rental property involves endless small tasks. While it's tempting to DIY everything to save a few bucks, some jobs require a professional — and not just for quality, but for safety and legal compliance.

Here's our guide to knowing when to roll up your sleeves and when to pick up the phone.

## ✅ DIY-Friendly Tasks

These are safe, simple, and don't require special licensing:

- **Air filter replacement** (every 3 months)
- **Battery replacement** in smoke/CO detectors
- **Gutter cleaning** (if comfortable with heights and have proper safety gear)
- **Landscaping** (mowing, trimming, weeding)
- **Caulking** around tubs and showers
- **Toilet plunging** (obviously)
- **Replacing light bulbs** (but consider LED upgrades for efficiency)
- **Testing GFCI outlets** (use the test button)
- **Checking water pressure** (simple gauge attachment)
- **Unclogging drains** with a plunger or hand snake

**Rule of thumb:** If it doesn't involve electricity, gas, plumbing inside walls, or structural elements, it's likely DIY-able.

## 🚨 ALWAYS Call a Licensed Pro

These tasks require permits, specialized training, or carry significant risk:

### Electrical Work
Any work inside electrical panels, wiring, or permanent fixtures. Mistakes cause fires, electrocution, and insurance denial. Hire a licensed electrician.

### Plumbing Inside Walls
Fixing leaks behind drywall, repiping, moving plumbing, or water heater installation. Improper work causes water damage and mold.

### HVAC Repairs
Furnace, boiler, and AC repairs involve refrigerants, combustion gases, and high-pressure systems. Licensed technicians only.

### Roofing
Even a simple shingle repair requires proper nailing patterns, underlayment, and flashing knowledge. One mistake leads to leaks.

### Gas Lines
Any work on gas lines or gas appliances must be done by a licensed professional. Gas leaks are explosive.

### Structural Changes
Anything that load-bearing walls, foundations, or major structural elements requires engineering and permits.

## The Hidden Costs of DIY Gone Wrong

You might think you're saving $300 on a plumber, but consider:

- **Time cost:** A pro does it in 1 hour; you spend 4 hours researching, buying tools, and fixing mistakes
- **Damage cost:** A botched DIY plumbing fix floods the unit → $5,000+ damage
- **Insurance implications:** Some DIY work voids homeowner's insurance if it causes damage
- **Code violations:** Unpermitted work can cause issues when selling or refinancing
- **Liability:** If a tenant gets hurt due to your shoddy work, you're liable
