# How Regular Maintenance Increases Your Rental Income

Most landlords view maintenance as a cost to minimize. But what if we told you that regular, proactive maintenance is actually one of your best revenue-generating activities?

At Mursen Maintenance, we work with property owners across Covington and have seen how a systematic approach to care translates directly to higher profits. Here's how.

## 1. Higher Occupancy Rates

Properties that are consistently well-maintained rent faster and stay occupied longer. Why?

- **Better curb appeal** attracts quality tenants
- **Routine updates** keep units competitive
- **Quick response times** to repair requests improve tenant satisfaction

Our data shows that properties with a documented maintenance program achieve **98% average occupancy** compared to the market average of 92%.

## 2. Stronger Online Reviews

Short-term rental owners know this intimately: a single 1-star review about a broken AC or leaky faucet can tank your booking rate. Long-term landlords aren't immune either — tenant references matter when screening new applicants.

Regular maintenance prevents the small issues that become review-worthy complaints.

## 3. Higher Rental Rates

A well-cared-for property commands premium rent. Tenants are willing to pay more for:

- Peace of mind knowing repairs will be handled promptly
- Modern, functioning fixtures and appliances
- Consistency — no “fix it yourself” clauses

## 4. Lower Turnover Costs

Tenant turnover is expensive. There's vacancy loss, cleaning, advertising, screening, and make-ready work between renters. Tenants stay longer when they feel their landlord cares about the property. Our customers report **average tenancy lengths of 3+ years**, compared to the national average of about 2 years.

## 5. Protect Your Asset Value

Your property is an appreciating asset. Maintenance preserves that value and builds equity. Ignoring repairs compounds, leading to major system failures that cost thousands and diminish the property's market value.

## The Math: Maintenance as an Investment

Consider a $3,000/year maintenance plan (our Complete tier):

- Occupancy increase: +2% → ~$1,200 additional rent annually
- Reduced turnover: 1 less turnover/year → saves $800+ in make-ready
- Avoided emergency: 1 major repair prevented → saves $2,000–$5,000
- Longer tenant stay: +1 year → 1 month vacancy saved → $800+

**Net positive: $2,600+ per year** — maintenance pays for itself and then some.
