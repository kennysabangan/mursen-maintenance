# Winterizing Your Rental: A Complete Landlord Guide

Winter doesn't ask permission — it arrives whether you're prepared or not. For property owners, winter is the riskiest season. A single frozen pipe can cause $5,000–$10,000 in water damage. Heating failures can lead to burst pipes, tenant displacement, and liability issues.

This guide covers everything you need to do before the first freeze hits.

## Why Winterization Matters

- **Frozen pipes** cause 1 in 5 winter property damage claims
- **Ice dams** can lead to roof leaks and interior water damage
- **Heating failures** in Kentucky winters risk both property and tenant safety
- **Drafts** increase energy bills by up to 20%

## The Winterization Checklist

### Plumbing Protection

- [ ] Insulate exposed pipes in attics, crawl spaces, and exterior walls
- [ ] Let faucets drip during极端 cold snaps
- [ ] Drain and shut off outdoor irrigation systems
- [ ] Install freeze sensors on vulnerable pipes
- [ ] Warn tenants about leaving heat on when away (minimum 55°F)

### Heating System Service

- [ ] Schedule professional furnace inspection (October/November)
- [ ] Replace air filters
- [ ] Check carbon monoxide detectors
- [ ] Verify heating vents are unblocked
- [ ] Ensure tenants know how to operate the thermostat

### Exterior Prep

- [ ] Clean gutters to prevent ice dams
- [ ] Install gutter guards if not already present
- [ ] Seal gaps around windows and doors
- [ ] Check roof for damaged shingles
- [ ] Trim tree branches that could fall on roof during ice storms

### Interior Comfort & Safety

- [ ] Verify smoke and CO detectors are functional
- [ ] Provide tenants with emergency contact info
- [ ] Stock properties with ice melt and sand (if HOA allows)
- [ ] Consider a generator for critical systems (for larger buildings)

## Tenant Communication

Many issues can be prevented by educating your tenants:

- Remind them to report heating problems immediately
- Ask them to keep cabinet doors open on exterior walls during cold snaps
- Encourage them to leave heat on when away during winter months
- Provide a clear emergency contact line
