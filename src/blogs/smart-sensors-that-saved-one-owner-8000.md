# Smart Sensors That Saved One Property Owner $8,000

It was a Tuesday morning when John S., a landlord in Latonia, received an alert on his phone: “Water leak detected in basement – upstairs bathroom.” He called his regular handyman, who arrived within 45 minutes and found a leaking toilet supply line. The fix took 20 minutes and cost $120 in parts.

The potential damage? The insurance adjuster later estimated that if the leak had gone undetected for another 24–48 hours, they'd be looking at $8,000+ in water damage, mold remediation, and tenant displacement costs.

This real story illustrates the power of smart home sensors for rental properties.

## The Hidden Dangers of Rental Properties

As a landlord, you can't be on-site 24/7. Tenants may not notice developing issues until it's too late:

- A slow leak under a sink
- A failing water heater
- A toilet that runs constantly
- A sump pump that stops working

These silent problems compound quickly.

## Smart Sensor Options

The good news: modern IoT sensors are affordable, reliable, and easy to install. Here are the most valuable ones for rentals:

### 1. Water Leak Sensors ($25–$50 each)

Place them:

- Under bathroom vanities
- Behind toilets
- Near water heaters
- In basements near sump pumps
- Under kitchen sinks

They detect moisture and send instant alerts to your phone. Most relay via WiFi and have battery backup.

### 2. Temperature & Freeze Sensors ($35–$60)

Alert you when temperatures drop below a threshold (set to 40°F). Crucial for:

- Preventing frozen pipes
- Ensuring heating is working during cold snaps
- Verifying tenant isn't turning heat off entirely

### 3. Smart Thermostats ($150–$250)

These do double duty:

- Allow remote temperature monitoring and adjustment
- Track heating/cooling runtime (can flag system issues)
- Some include leak detection (like Ecobee's optional sensor)

### 4. Smoke & CO Detectors with Remote Alerts ($60–$100)

Traditional detectors only sound locally. Smart versions send alerts to your phone when tripped — potentially saving lives and property.

### 5. Sump Pump Monitors ($60–$120)

These detect pump activation and can alert you if:

- The pump runs continuously (high groundwater)
- The pump fails during a storm
- Power is lost (if battery-backed)

## Cost vs Benefit

A full sensor suite for a typical single-family rental:

- 4× water leak sensors: $120
- 2× temperature sensors: $80
- Smart thermostat: $200
- Smart smoke/CO: $80
- Sump pump monitor: $80

**Total: ~$560** — less than one typical insurance deductible.

Meanwhile, the average water damage claim for landlords is ~$5,000–$10,000.

## Installation & Tenant Considerations

- Most sensors are DIY peel-and-stick with simple apps
- Tenants generally don't mind if sensors are non-intrusive and privacy-focused (they alert you, not the tenant)
- Disclosure requirements vary by state — check local landlord-tenant laws
- Consider offering a small rent discount in exchange for allowing sensors
