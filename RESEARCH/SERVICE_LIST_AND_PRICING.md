## Pricing Strategy Notes

1. **À la carte is the funnel, not the margin driver.** Price toward the lower-middle of the market range to win initial jobs from Nextdoor/social. Convert to subscriptions after 2–3 visits.

2. **Subscriptions are the business.** Monthly recurring revenue from $199–$349/mo per customer is where margins live. À la carte exists to feed the funnel.

3. **Handyman hourly is slightly below market ($75 vs $85)** to win Nextdoor leads. Once they trust you, the subscription pitch is easy.

4. **Lawn prices are right at market ($45 vs $47 average).** Don't undercut here — lawn is a service people expect to pay standard rates for.

5. **Window and power wash can be priced slightly below market** because these are high-margin, low-labor services that differentiate Mursen from handyman-only competitors.

6. **Property investor pricing should be aggressive** ($249/unit for 3+ units). One sign-up = 5–20 units. LTV is massive even at lower per-unit pricing.
